Part 1

Part 2

I copy/pasted letter path from Part 1 into a defs sections. I set the fill attribute of the path an "inherit" in order to customize this value for each of the two instances. Then I draw a simple rectangle container. I linked two instances of the same defined letter path. Each of the two letter are customized through rotation, translation and scale transformations.

For the rotation:

  • the instance for the letter at the left is rotated 15 degrees clockwise, having the rotation axis on the top left corner;
  • the instance for the letter at the left is rotated 15 degrees counter clockwise, having the rotation axis on the top right corner.

For the translation, I first translated the first letter to make its top-left corner coincide with the top-left corner of the rectange. At this point, I found that a scale factor of 0.9 is appropriate to make the letter bound into the rectangle. Then, I translated the second letter to make it fit.

Finally, I've defnied a viewbox and a reduced width in order to scale down the whole SVG image, to match the width of 299px defined for the PNG picture of the assignement.

Part 3

I added a background gradient in 5 steps: red at offset 0, yellow at offset 0.25, green at offset 0.5, and one more time yellow at offset 0.75 and red at offset 1.

I had the feeling that the "flat ellipse" cannot be drawn with an ellipse, so I choosed to use a path with quadratic beziers ('Q'). The two elipses are defined in the same path, in order to benefit the "evenodd" fill rule to make the center emty and transparent. The gradient is radial. I made it start from green at 0.4 offset, as the center of the ellipse is invisible, and defined orange at 0.8 offset and finally red at 1 offset.
